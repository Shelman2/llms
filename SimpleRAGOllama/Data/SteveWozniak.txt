Stephen Gary Wozniak, also known as "Woz," was born on August 11, 1950, in San Jose, California. He is an American electronics engineer, programmer, philanthropist, and technology entrepreneur who co-founded Apple Inc. in 1976 alongside Steve Jobs and Ronald Wayne.

Key Achievements:

- 1976: Designed the Apple I, Apple's first product, single-handedly.
- 1977: Developed the Apple II, one of the first highly successful mass-produced microcomputers.
- 1985: Left Apple Inc. but retained a ceremonial role.

Known for his innovative engineering skills, playful personality, and generosity, Wozniak played a pivotal role in the personal computer revolution. After leaving Apple, he has focused on philanthropy, technology advocacy, and education.
